Dallas Seavey Has Knack for Winning Iditarod With Few Dogs

Seavey set many records competing in the Iditarod this year. His victory marked his third consecutive win and fourth in five years, losing only to his father, and he also set a new record by finishing the race in the fastest time. But the more interesting record he broke involved the number of dogs he crossed the finish line with. After beginning the 1,000 mile race across Alaska with 16 dogs, Seavey crossed the finish line with just six dogs, erasing the record he set the year before finish the race with seven dogs. How Dogs Can Sniff Out Diabetes and Cancer

Studies have shown that dogs are able to detect a variety of cancers and other diseases in their owners. It is unclear what it is they can smell, but pets have been able to detect drops in blood sugar and dangerous bacteria in urine samples. Ten dogs rescued from Korean meat farm available for adoption

Back in January, the Humane Society International shut down a meat farm in South Korea and rescued some 250 animals. They were packed in filthy cages with little food or veterinary care. Some of the rescued dogs, of a variety of breeds, were transported to San Francisco where they were treated by professionals and rehabilitated. 12,400-Year-Old Frozen Dog Thawed for Research

Scientists recently thawed out a frozen dog that was found in the ice of Siberia. The specimen is believed to be over 12,000 years old and there is hope that a lot of knowledge to be gained from the frozen canine. It is thought to be a now-extinct species, and it was so well preserved in the ice that it still has a fully intact brain. This is by far one of the best preserved ancient dogs ever discovered.
